Oliver Kell System + Stage Analysis Framework

üéØ Core Trading Rules - Oliver Kell System

1Put Risk First
Always define your risk before entering. Know your stop loss, position size, and maximum loss tolerance. Risk management trumps profit potential.
2Plan Your Trade
If you fail to plan, you are planning to fail. Document entry, stop, target, and thesis before trade execution.
3Trust Your Stops
Don't trust your stocks, trust your stops. Honor your stop losses without hesitation. The market doesn't care about your opinion.
4Better Out Than In
Better to be out and want in, than in and want out. When in doubt, reduce position or exit. You can always re-enter.
5P&L Guides Aggression
Let your P&L guide your level of aggression. When winning, increase size carefully. When losing, reduce size and go back to basics.
6Only Price Pays
Opinions, news, and narratives don't matter. Only price action pays. Follow the chart, not the story.
7Sell Into Strength
Sell into strength or you will sell into weakness. Take profits when the stock is moving up, not when it's collapsing.
8Failed Moves = Fast Moves
From failed moves come fast moves. When a breakout fails, expect rapid reversal. When support breaks, downside accelerates.
9No Earnings Gambles
Don't gamble on earnings. Options decay accelerates and IV crush is real. Either exit before earnings or size very small.
10No Extended Stocks
Don't buy extended stocks. First third of move = best risk/reward. Wait for consolidation and next clean setup.
11Sell Losers Quickly
Sell your losers quickly, always. Small losses are acceptable, big losses destroy accounts. Cut fast, cut clean.
12Never Average Down
Never average down on options. Only average up on winners. Losers get smaller, winners get bigger.

üìà Stage Analysis Entry Rules

Stage 1BBasing Completion - High Risk

  • Entry Signal: Stock breaking above 10-week SMA with volume, forming higher lows
  • Confirmation: 10-week crosses above 40-week, weekly chart showing accumulation
  • Options Strategy: Small position ATM calls, 45-60 DTE (Days to Expiration)
  • Stop Loss: Below most recent swing low or base breakdown (-7% to -10%)
  • Risk Level: Highest risk - use smallest position size (25-30% normal size)

Stage 2Advancing Phase - Best Risk/Reward

  • Entry Signal: Breakout above Stage 1 resistance on strong volume, clean 10-EMA & 20-EMA crossback
  • Confirmation: Higher highs, higher lows, trading above 50-day SMA, RS line improving
  • Options Strategy: Full position ATM or slightly ITM calls, 30-60 DTE
  • Stop Loss: Below breakout pivot or 20-day EMA (-4% to -7%)
  • Risk Level: Optimal - first third of Stage 2 offers best risk/reward

Stage 2 ContinuationMid-Stage Pullback

  • Entry Signal: Pullback to 10-day, 20-day, or 50-day SMA, wedge pop formation
  • Confirmation: Volume dries up on pullback, increases on reversal, EMAs still rising
  • Options Strategy: Starter position, scale up if working, 30-45 DTE
  • Stop Loss: Below pullback low or key moving average (-5% to -8%)
  • Risk Level: Medium - depends on extension from base

Stage 3Topping Phase - AVOID

  • Characteristics: Choppy price action, 10-week crossing below 40-week, distribution
  • Action: Exit longs, take profits, move stops to cost. No new long entries.
  • Options Strategy: Close calls, consider puts for short setup if clear Stage 4 developing

Stage 4Declining Phase - SHORT ONLY

  • Entry Signal: Breakdown below Stage 3 support, 10-week clearly below 40-week
  • Confirmation: Lower highs, lower lows, below 50-day and 200-day, RS line weak
  • Options Strategy: ATM or slightly OTM puts, 30-60 DTE
  • Stop Loss: Above recent swing high or breakdown resistance (+5% to +8%)

‚öôÔ∏è Options-Specific Rules

Contract Selection

  • Time Decay: 30-60 DTE for swing trades, 45-90 DTE for position trades
  • Strike Selection: ATM or slightly ITM for lower risk, OTM for aggressive plays
  • Liquidity: Minimum 100 daily volume, tight bid-ask spread (under 10% of premium)
  • Weekly Options: See "Weekly Trading Rules" section below for small account strategy

Position Sizing

  • Max Risk Per Trade: 1-2% of account on any single options trade
  • Options Allocation: No more than 20-30% of portfolio in options at once
  • Scale In: Start with 1/3 position, add on confirmation
  • Never Go Full Size: Options volatility demands smaller sizing

Profit Taking Strategy

  • Free Roll Target: Sell 1/3 to 1/2 at +50% to +100% to recover cost basis
  • Scale Out: Take 1/3 at R1 target, 1/3 at R2, let 1/3 run with trailing stop
  • Time-Based Exit: Consider exiting at 14 DTE even if winning (theta acceleration)
  • IV Crush Awareness: Exit before earnings or major catalyst events

‚ö° Weekly Options Trading Rules (Small Account Builder)

‚ö†Ô∏è High Risk Strategy: Weeklies have extreme theta decay and require precise timing. Only use with strict discipline and small position sizing.

When to Trade Weeklies

  • Account Size: Under $5,000 where 30+ DTE contracts are too expensive
  • Strong Conviction Only: Clear Stage 2 momentum plays with catalyst (not random speculation)
  • Short Hold Time: Plan to hold 1-3 days maximum, not full week
  • High Probability Setups: Bull flags, wedge pops, clean breakouts with volume

Weekly Options Entry Rules

  • Monday/Tuesday Entry: Enter early week (Mon-Tue), avoid Thursday/Friday entries
  • Stage 2 Confirmed: Stock must be in clear Stage 2, above 10/20 EMA, 50-day rising
  • Catalyst Present: Sector rotation, market strength, breakout in progress
  • Volume Confirmation: Above-average volume on entry day (1.5x+ daily average)
  • Strike Selection: ATM or 1 strike OTM maximum (avoid deep OTM lottery tickets)
  • Expiration: Current week or next week only (5-12 days to expiration)

Position Sizing for Weeklies

  • Max Risk: 1-2% of total account per trade (same as regular options)
  • Smaller Contract Count: Buy fewer contracts than you think (they move fast)
  • No All-In: Never use more than 20% of account on single weekly trade
  • Scale In Forbidden: Enter full position at once (no time to scale with weeklies)

Exit Strategy for Weeklies (CRITICAL)

  • +30% Rule: Take profit at +30% minimum. Don't get greedy with theta decay.
  • +50% Auto-Sell: Sell at least 50% of position at +50% gain
  • +100% Exit All: If you hit +100%, sell everything immediately (rare, don't push it)
  • Daily Close Rule: If trade not working by market close Day 1, consider exiting
  • No Overnight Holding (Optional): For very aggressive traders, close all weeklies EOD
  • Wednesday Exit: Exit all remaining positions by Wednesday close (theta accelerates Thursday/Friday)
  • Down 25% = Cut: If down 25% from entry, exit immediately no questions asked

Weekly Options DON'Ts

  • ‚ùå No Earnings Holds: NEVER hold weeklies through earnings (IV crush will destroy you)
  • ‚ùå No FOMC/Fed Days: Avoid weeklies on major Fed announcement days
  • ‚ùå No Averaging Down: If weekly goes against you, cut it. Never add to loser.
  • ‚ùå No Hoping: Don't hold losing weeklies hoping for recovery. Time decay is relentless.
  • ‚ùå No Extended Stocks: Don't buy weeklies on stocks already up 20%+ in short time
  • ‚ùå No Low Liquidity: Weekly contracts must have 50+ daily volume minimum

Building a Small Account with Weeklies

  • High Win Rate Focus: You need 60%+ win rate to overcome losses. Be selective.
  • Compound Wins: After 3 consecutive winners, increase position size by 25%
  • After Loss, Shrink: After any loss, reduce next position size by 50%
  • Track Weekly Results: Document every weekly trade separately from regular trades
  • Transition Goal: Once account reaches $5K-10K, transition to 30+ DTE contracts
  • Weekly Budget: Limit weekly trades to 2-3 per week maximum (overtrading kills)

Best Stocks for Weekly Options

  • High Volume: SPY, QQQ, AAPL, TSLA, NVDA, AMD, MSFT (liquid options)
  • High IV: Stocks with elevated implied volatility (options move more)
  • Clear Trends: Strong Stage 2 momentum stocks, not choppy Stage 3
  • Sector Leaders: Leading stocks in leading sectors (use Finviz screener)

üõë Webull Stop Loss Practices

Initial Stop Loss (Entry)

  • Set Immediately: Place stop order the second you enter position (separate order window)
  • Stop Type: Use LIMIT orders, not market orders (prevents slippage on options)
  • Stage 2 Breakout: Stop at -5% to -7% below breakout point or pivot low
  • Stage 2 Pullback: Stop at -5% to -8% below entry or key moving average
  • Stage 1B/Early: Wider stop -7% to -10% (more volatile)

Trailing Stops on Webull (For Winners)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Webull trailing stops are DAY orders only, NOT GTC. You must reset them daily.

How to Set Trailing Stop on Webull:

  1. Open position ‚Üí Click "Sell" ‚Üí Select "Trailing Stop"
  2. Choose between $ amount or % trailing (recommend % for options)
  3. Set trail percentage: 15-20% for options volatility
  4. Set daily before market open (trailing stops don't carry overnight)

Trailing Stop Strategy:

  • Once +25% profit: Move stop to breakeven (cost basis)
  • Once +50% profit: Lock in +25% with trailing stop
  • Once +100% profit: Take 1/3 to 1/2 off, trail remainder at 15-20%
  • For multi-day holds: Trail at 10-day EMA or 20-day EMA (check daily)
  • For runners: Switch to manual stops at key support levels vs. trailing %

Manual Stop Adjustments (Alternative to Trailing)

  • Daily Task: Adjust stop loss every morning based on previous day's action
  • Stage 2 In Motion: Raise stop to below prior day low or last support
  • Use Technical Levels: Trail below 10-EMA (aggressive) or 20-EMA (conservative)
  • Never Lower Stops: Stops only move up on winners, never down

Emergency Exit Rules

  • 20-EMA Break: If stock decisively loses 20-day EMA, exit immediately
  • Stage Change: Any sign of Stage 2 ‚Üí Stage 3 transition, exit
  • Market Crash: If SPY breaks major support, close all options immediately
  • Down 20%: If option down 20% from entry, exit no excuses (stop didn't fill)

‚úÖ Pre-Trade Checklist

  • Stock is in Stage 2 (advancing) or clear Stage 2 continuation setup
  • 10-day EMA > 20-day EMA, price above both
  • 50-day SMA rising, price above 50-day
  • Volume confirms breakout or reversal (above 20-day avg)
  • Relative strength vs. SPY is positive or improving
  • Not extended (within 15% of 50-day SMA)
  • Options contract has sufficient liquidity (100+ daily volume)
  • Bid-ask spread is tight (under 10% of premium)
  • 30+ DTE remaining on contract
  • No earnings within next 7 days
  • SPY/QQQ in Stage 2 or at minimum not breaking down
  • Stop loss level defined (price point)
  • Position size calculated (1-2% max risk)
  • Profit targets defined (R1, R2, runner targets)
  • Trade thesis written in journal before entry
